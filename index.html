Techstravaganza 2021 Information

Overview

Techstravaganza 2021 will be hosted synchronously online. The kids will be at home and able to navigate through our website to watch different demonstrations and interact with the exhibitors (Thomas Jefferson HS clubs, Thomas Jefferson HS exhibitors, and external exhibitors). Please keep in mind that our main mission is bringing STEM to low-income communities and a lot of the students participating will be from Title 1 schools.


Schedule

Exhibitors will receive a window on the day where they will demonstrate their 10-minute-activity for FOUR iterations. Each iteration of activities will have a 5 minute break in between to reset your activity and get ready for the next iteration.

For an example of what the event schedule will look like, please see the link here. There are 3 shifts in the day, and 4 iterations in a shift. Kids will be able to choose among any of the exhibitors in every iteration, resulting in a viewing total of 12 different demos per kid.

About Techstrav Who Can Attend? Why Come?
Techstravaganza is a free, open to the public hands-on STEM activity fair that attracts thousands of visitors from the DMV area each year. This past year, we had over 5000 visitors from over 192 different schools.

Each year, we have over 300 TJ student volunteers and over a dozen exhibiting companies who help us make this event possible. 		This event is open to children of all ages! However, our labs do specifically target elementary and middle school students.

Techstravaganza is also a great family event. Not only do the children walk away having learned something new, the parents do to! 		At Techstravaganza, you will find over 50 different labs and demonstrations that cover a wide range of STEM concepts so there is something for everyone!

Some of our most popular labs include Banana DNA, Making Ice Cream, and Stomp Rockets!

Who Runs It? Why Register?
TWIST (Tomorrow's Women in Science and Technology) organizes the event and is a student-run STEM outreach organization at TJHSST.

Our mission is to encourage students from diverse and low-income backgrounds to pursue STEM subjects.

We aim to increase female enrollment in traditionally underrepresented TJ research labs by empowering girls within our school community to pursue careers in these fields.		 By registering, we are able to get a better estimate of how many lab materials to prepare for the day.

In addition, this signs you up for our email list so we can send out notifications regarding logistics. We also use EventBrite to check in visitors so this helps speed up the signin process.

Tickets are free because we want this event to be accessible to everyone.
